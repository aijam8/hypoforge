# Equilibrium Domain Spacing in Lamellar Block Copolymer Thin Films is Set Thermodynamically

**Preprint — Soft Matter Letters (2024).** *Consensus review.*

## Abstract

We review the determinants of lamellar domain spacing $L_0$ in diblock copolymer
thin films. Across the surveyed literature, $L_0$ is governed by the
**thermodynamic** balance of chain stretching and interfacial energy, scaling as
$L_0 \sim a N^{2/3} \chi^{1/6}$. Because $\chi$ is temperature-dependent, the
**domain spacing is a function of annealing temperature alone** at equilibrium.

## Key claim (consensus)

> Once a film is annealed above its order–disorder transition and re-cooled, the
> equilibrium domain spacing depends **only on temperature** through $\chi(T)$.
> Processing variables such as applied shear set orientation but **do not alter the
> equilibrium spacing** — any apparent shear dependence is a transient,
> non-equilibrium artifact that relaxes out on annealing.

## Implication

Reported deviations of $L_0$ under steady shear are widely attributed to
incomplete annealing or measurement error, not to a genuine shear contribution to
the equilibrium spacing. The field therefore treats $L_0(T)$ as the complete model.
